No Experience Beyond the Five Aggregates - Discussion

Nikolas

Greeting friends,

In my discussions with representatives of the eternalist wing in both Theravāda and Mahāyāna traditions, I've observed a recurring pattern in their arguments. At the core lies a central idea: the attempt to isolate or elevate a purified, transcendent aspect of personal existence—something presumably distinct from the general aggregates, sense bases, or elements.

Thus, one of the most important questions for a correct understanding of the Dhamma is the firm recognition that the Buddha's teaching on the five khandhas (aggregates), ayatanas (sense bases), and dhatus (elements) is holistic—that it encompasses all aspects of experience without exception. It is not merely a description of the "lower," defiled, or dualistic part of reality, contrasted with some supposed higher, perfect, or even "absolute" layer that somehow escapes the framework of conditioned phenomena (dhammas).

The aim of this post is to invite thoughtful responses and explore rational arguments and scriptural sources that can reliably affirm the holistic nature of the Buddha's teaching regarding the categories of experience and existence (aggregates, sense bases, elements, etc.).
